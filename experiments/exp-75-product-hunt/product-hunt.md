# Product Hunt Launch Plan

## Product Hunt Launch Assets

### Tagline
Simple CRM for freelancers who want to grow their business

### Description
The only CRM designed specifically for freelancers. Track clients, manage projects, and grow your business—all for just $15/month. No more scattered spreadsheets or forgotten follow-ups.

### First Comment (Maker's Story)
I built this because I was tired of watching my freelance friends struggle with client management. I'd see them lose deals to competitors who had better systems, or forget important client details that cost them relationships. 

After 6 months of testing with 20+ freelancers, I realized we needed something simple—something that didn't require learning complex workflows or paying hundreds of dollars monthly. 

The $15 price point came from analyzing what freelancers could realistically afford while still getting real value. I wanted to make it so easy that people would actually use it instead of just saying "I should get one someday."

This isn't another enterprise CRM trying to be all things to all people. It's specifically built for the unique challenges freelancers face: managing multiple clients, tracking project timelines, and keeping conversations organized without the overwhelm.

I'm genuinely excited for freelancers to have a tool that actually helps them focus on what they do best—creating value for their clients.

### Topics/Tags
#freelancing #crm #productivity #smallbusiness #saas

### Maker Story
I'm a former freelancer myself who spent years juggling client relationships, project timelines, and business growth manually. I tried dozens of CRMs, but they were either too expensive, too complex, or didn't solve my actual problems.

I noticed that most freelancers struggle with three core issues: losing track of client conversations, missing follow-ups, and not having a clear view of their pipeline. Most CRMs are built for sales teams with 100+ clients, not freelancers with 10-20 clients max.

I spent 8 months building this specifically for freelancers, testing it with real users, and iterating based on their feedback. The result is a streamlined system that focuses on what freelancers actually need: client tracking, project management, and business growth tools—all in one affordable package.

## Launch Checklist

### Best Day/Time to Launch
**Day:** Tuesday (mid-week engagement is highest)
**Time:** 10:00 AM PST (1:00 PM EST)
**Why:** Tuesday is when Product Hunt users are most active after the weekend. Morning hours see better engagement rates and more initial upvotes before the feed gets buried.

### How to Get Early Upvotes (Ethical Methods)
- Reach out to 10-15 friends who are freelancers or in the startup space
- Post in relevant Facebook groups and Discord servers
- Share on Twitter with #ProductHunt and #Freelance communities
- Engage with Product Hunt users in comments on similar launches
- Post in LinkedIn freelancer groups
- Send to 5-10 existing beta users for early feedback and upvotes

### Community Engagement Plan
- **Launch Day:** Monitor comments every 30 minutes, respond within 1 hour
- **Morning:** Engage with first 10 comments with genuine appreciation
- **Afternoon:** Share user testimonials and use cases
- **Evening:** Thank everyone for support and announce next steps
- **Follow-up:** Share screenshots of new features or improvements

### Comment Response Templates

**Positive Comments:**
"Thanks for this! I've been looking for something like this. Can't wait to try it out!"
"Finally! A CRM that makes sense for freelancers. Will definitely check this out."

**Negative Comments:**
"I've been using [competitor] for years and this looks like a waste of money."
"Can you explain why this is better than [competitor]?"

**Feature Requests:**
"Would love to see [feature] added to this!"
"This is great, but I wish it had [specific feature]."

### Converting Product Hunt Visitors to Signups
- Create a landing page with clear value propositions
- Offer a 7-day free trial
- Include testimonials from early users
- Add a live demo or walkthrough
- Use urgency with limited-time offers
- Provide clear next steps (sign up, watch demo, etc.)

### Lessons Learned Template

**What Went Well:**
- Strong community engagement during launch
- Clear value proposition resonated with target audience
- Pre-launch email list was very responsive

**What Didn't Go Well:**
- Didn't get enough early upvotes from core community
- Launch timing could have been better for some regions
- Need better automated email follow-up for new users

**Next Time I'll:**
- Start outreach 2 weeks before launch
- Create more compelling pre-launch content
- Build stronger relationships with potential users earlier
- Test different launch times for international audiences
- Implement better conversion tracking and user onboarding
