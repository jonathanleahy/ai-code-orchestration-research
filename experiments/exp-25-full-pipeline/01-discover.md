# Customer Discovery

# User Personas for Invoice Generator

## Persona 1: Sarah Chen - Solo Freelance Designer
**Role:** Graphic Designer & Illustrator  
**Background:** 5 years of freelance experience, works from home, serves clients globally  
**Current Solution:** Excel templates + manual PDF creation  
**Top 3 Pain Points:** 
1. Time spent on administrative tasks instead of creative work
2. Manual data entry prone to errors
3. Difficulty tracking payments and follow-ups  
**Budget:** $20-30/month

### Interview Answers

**Current invoicing process:** "I use a basic Excel template that I've customized over the years. I create a new sheet for each client, manually input the hours, rates, and calculate totals. Then I save it as a PDF and email it. It's really inefficient and I often forget to send them or send them late."

**Biggest frustration:** "The biggest frustration is that I spend 2-3 hours every month just on invoicing and chasing payments. I'm a creative person, not an accountant, so this administrative burden takes away from my actual work."

**Must-have features:** "1) Auto-calculation of totals and taxes, 2) Client database with payment history, 3) Automated follow-ups for overdue invoices"

**Essential integrations:** "QuickBooks for accounting, Google Drive for storage, and maybe a calendar integration to track when I need to send invoices."

**Would you pay $15/month?** "Absolutely! I'd pay $20 if it saved me just 2 hours a month. That's worth it for my time."

**One feature to build first:** "Auto-fill client information from previous projects. I work with the same clients repeatedly, so having their details pre-filled would save me so much time."

## Persona 2: Marcus Rodriguez - Agency Owner
**Role:** Co-founder & Creative Director, 8-person design agency  
**Background:** 10 years in creative industry, manages multiple projects and clients  
**Current Solution:** QuickBooks + custom Google Sheets  
**Top 3 Pain Points:** 
1. Multiple clients with different billing terms
2. Complex project tracking across teams
3. Inconsistent payment collection  
**Budget:** $50-100/month

### Interview Answers

**Current invoicing process:** "We use QuickBooks for accounting but create invoices manually in Google Sheets. Each project manager creates their own invoice based on hours tracked in our project management tool. It's a mess. We have 8 different people creating invoices, so there's no consistency."

**Biggest frustration:** "The biggest frustration is the lack of visibility into our cash flow. We don't know who owes us money until we get a phone call. We also lose track of which projects are invoiced and which aren't."

**Must-have features:** "1) Team collaboration with role-based access, 2) Project-based invoicing with time tracking integration, 3) Automated payment reminders and status tracking"

**Essential integrations:** "QuickBooks, Trello/Asana for project management, Stripe for payments, and Slack for team communication."

**Would you pay $15/month?** "That's a great price point for us! We're looking at $50-100/month for something that actually solves our problems. I'd definitely consider it if it's reliable."

**One feature to build first:** "Project-based invoicing with time tracking integration. We need to be able to create invoices directly from project data and track time spent on each client's work."

## Persona 3: Jennifer Park - Freelance Accountant
**Role:** Bookkeeper & Tax Consultant  
**Background:** 8 years experience, works with 50+ freelancers and small businesses  
**Current Solution:** FreshBooks + manual adjustments  
**Top 3 Pain Points:** 
1. Managing multiple client accounts and invoices
2. Ensuring compliance with tax regulations
3. Time spent on client follow-ups  
**Budget:** $30-50/month

### Interview Answers

**Current invoicing process:** "I use FreshBooks for most of my clients, but I still have to manually adjust invoices for tax purposes and ensure compliance. I also have to chase payments and send reminders. It's a lot of back-and-forth with clients who don't understand the invoicing process."

**Biggest frustration:** "The biggest frustration is that I spend too much time on administrative tasks instead of helping my clients with their actual financial planning. I also get complaints from clients who say their invoices are confusing or missing information."

**Must-have features:** "1) Tax-compliant invoice templates, 2) Client communication dashboard, 3) Automated compliance checking and reporting"

**Essential integrations:** "QuickBooks Online, Xero for accounting, email integration, and possibly a client portal for self-service access."

**Would you pay $15/month?** "I'd pay $25/month if it helped me reduce my administrative time by even 2 hours a week. I'm always looking for tools that can help me scale my services."

**One feature to build first:** "Tax-compliant invoice templates with automatic tax calculation. My clients are often confused about what they're being charged for, and I want to eliminate that confusion with clear, compliant invoices."

---

## Summary of Key Insights

**Solo Freelancer (Sarah):** Values simplicity and time-saving features. Willing to pay for automation that reduces manual work.

**Agency Owner (Marcus):** Needs team collaboration and project management integration. Looking for comprehensive solutions that streamline workflows.

**Accountant (Jennifer):** Prioritizes compliance and client communication. Seeks tools that reduce administrative burden while maintaining professional standards.

Each persona represents a different level of complexity and budget, providing clear direction for feature prioritization and pricing strategy.
