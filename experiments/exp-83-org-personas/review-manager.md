# Manager (Team Lead)

**1. Does this CRM serve YOUR needs? (YES/PARTIALLY/NO)**

**PARTIALLY** - It's a decent starting point but falls flat on several key areas I need as a team lead.

**2. What's the #1 thing you need that's MISSING?**

**Team assignment tracking and workload visibility** - I need to see at a glance who's working on what, who's overloaded, and who's idle. The current activity log is too fragmented. I want a "who's doing what" view that shows me my team's current projects, deadlines, and capacity. Without this, I'm constantly chasing people to see what they're working on.

**3. What feature would you use MOST?**

**The dashboard** - but only if I could customize it to show team metrics, revenue by person, and client status. Right now it's generic and doesn't tell me what I actually need to manage my team. I'd want to see: revenue trends, team performance scores, client activity by rep, and overdue tasks.

**4. What feature is USELESS to you?**

**Simple invoicing** - This is a huge waste of space. As a team lead, I don't create invoices, I don't send them, and I don't track payments. My team handles that. The invoicing system is just cluttering the interface and making it harder to find what I actually need.

**5. Would you recommend this to a colleague at your level? Why/why not?**

**No, not yet.** It's too basic for team management. I'd recommend it to a solo freelancer or a junior team member who just needs basic client management, but not for someone managing a team of 5. It's like giving a chef a can opener instead of a full kitchen - it's not that it's bad, it's just not what you need to cook a meal.

**6. Rate 1-10 for YOUR use case.**

**3/10** - It's functional for basic client management but completely misses the mark on team management. I'd give it a 7/10 if it had proper team tracking features, but I'd need to add those myself through workarounds that would be more painful than just using a proper team management tool.

The core problem is it's built for individual freelancers, not team leads managing multiple people. It's like a car that only has a steering wheel and no pedals - you can technically move, but you're missing the whole point of the vehicle.
