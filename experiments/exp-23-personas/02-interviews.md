# Persona Interviews

### **User Interview – Maria (Freelance UX Designer)**  
**Interviewer:** Let’s start by walking through your current invoicing process.

**Maria:**  
I’ve got a set of Excel templates that I’ve customized over the years — they’re not pretty but they work. When a project wraps up, I’ll copy the template, fill in details like hours worked, rate per hour, total amount due, and then export it as a PDF to send via email. It’s kind of clunky because sometimes I have to go back and tweak things if clients ask for changes — which happens often. I also keep a separate Google Sheet where I track all my clients and their payment statuses, but honestly, it’s easy to lose track of who owes what when there are so many emails flying around.  

**Interviewer:** What’s your single biggest frustration?

**Maria:**  
It takes me 8+ hours a month just doing admin work — and that’s not even counting the time spent chasing down late payments or adjusting invoices after clients request changes. That’s time I could be spending designing or working on new projects. The worst part is when I get a client who asks for a last-minute change that means I need to redo the whole invoice from scratch. It feels like a waste of money and energy.

**Interviewer:** If this tool could do one magical thing, what would it be?

**Maria:**  
It would automatically generate and send an invoice based on time tracked in my calendar or project management tool, and then notify me when it's been paid. That way I wouldn’t have to manually create anything ever again unless there’s something special about the project.

**Interviewer:** What integrations are essential?

**Maria:**  
QuickBooks Online is a must — I already use it for taxes and expense tracking. Also Stripe for payments and maybe a way to integrate with Google Drive or Dropbox so I can store invoices without having to deal with emails or folders.

**Interviewer:** Would you pay $15/month? What must be included?

**Maria:**  
Yes, absolutely — especially if it saves me at least two hours a week. Must include customizable templates, automatic invoice creation from tracked time, and a mobile app that lets me send invoices while on the go.

**Interviewer:** What would make you trust this tool with financial data?

**Maria:**  
Security certifications like SSL encryption, two-factor authentication, and clear privacy policies. I don't want to risk losing sensitive info or getting hacked.

**Interviewer:** If I build one feature this month, what should it be?

**Maria:**  
Make it easy to add recurring invoices. I often have retainer clients who want monthly billing, and right now I’m just copying and pasting the same template every month.

### **User Interview – James (Freelance Web Developer)**  
**Interviewer:** Walk me through how you currently handle invoicing.

**James:**  
We use Wave mostly for basic bookkeeping and some manual Excel sheets for more complex stuff. I usually draft invoices in Microsoft Word, format them nicely, and then email them out. We also track time using Toggl, but we don’t really sync that into our invoicing system. So I end up entering hours manually into the invoice. It works, but it's slow and error-prone. Sometimes I forget to send the invoice right away, or I miss sending follow-ups. It’s frustrating when I see a client hasn’t paid even though we completed a project weeks ago.

**Interviewer:** What’s your single biggest frustration?

**James:**  
Inconsistent invoicing — sometimes I forget to send an invoice or forget to include certain charges. Then I end up chasing clients or having to explain why they haven’t been billed yet. Plus, I’m always worried about missing deadlines or forgetting to update payment status in spreadsheets. It’s not glamorous, but it eats up valuable time.

**Interviewer:** If this tool could do one magical thing, what would it be?

**James:**  
Automatically pull time logs from Toggl or Clockify and convert them into invoices with line items. That would save me hours every week and eliminate human errors.

**Interviewer:** What integrations are essential?

**James:**  
Toggl or Clockify for time tracking, QuickBooks or Xero for accounting, Stripe or PayPal for payments, and maybe Slack integration to notify me when an invoice gets paid.

**Interviewer:** Would you pay $15/month? What must be included?

**James:**  
Nope, that’s too low. I’d rather pay $40–$60/month if it meant full automation and team collaboration features. Need time tracking integration, branding options, and multi-user access so my team can log hours and submit invoices.

**Interviewer:** What would make you trust this tool with financial data?

**James:**  
Solid security practices, transparent data handling policies, and reliable customer support. Also, I'd want to know exactly where my data lives and whether it's backed up securely.

**Interviewer:** If I build one feature this month, what should it be?

**James:**  
Time-tracking integration with Toggl or Clockify. That alone would cut down on admin time significantly.

### **User Interview – Lisa (Freelance Content Writer)**  
**Interviewer:** Tell me about your current invoicing workflow.

**Lisa:**  
I use Google Docs templates that I’ve built up over time. They’re simple enough that I can quickly edit them for each client. Once a piece of content is delivered, I’ll open the template, plug in the word count, rate, and total, and then send it via email. I keep a spreadsheet to track which clients are paying on time and who’s dragging their feet. But honestly, it’s messy. It’s hard to remember which ones are late or have been paid, especially since I get so many emails and messages.

**Interviewer:** What’s your single biggest frustration?

**Lisa:**  
Cash flow problems because of inconsistent payment schedules. Some clients pay within a few days, others take months. It makes planning difficult and puts pressure on my personal finances. Also, I spend way too much time formatting invoices — it’s not glamorous, but it eats into my writing time.

**Interviewer:** If this tool could do one magical thing, what would it be?

**Lisa:**  
Generate invoices automatically from a list of completed projects and allow me to schedule recurring invoices for retainer clients. That way, I never miss a payment and can focus on writing.

**Interviewer:** What integrations are essential?

**Lisa:**  
Stripe or PayPal for payments, Google Drive for storage, and maybe Mailchimp or HubSpot for sending reminders. I don’t want to rely on email alone.

**Interviewer:** Would you pay $15/month? What must be included?

**Lisa:**  
Yes — but only if it's super simple and doesn’t require any setup. I don’t want to learn how to use a complicated dashboard. Just clean templates, auto-invoicing, and a quick way to send and track payments.

**Interviewer:** What would make you trust this tool with financial data?

**Lisa:**  
Clear terms of service, strong encryption, and good reviews from other writers. I don’t want to risk losing money or having my information stolen.

**Interviewer:** If I build one feature this month, what should it be?

**Lisa:**  
Recurring invoicing for retainer clients. That’s what keeps me from losing income and gives me peace of mind.

### **User Interview – David (Freelance Photographer)**  
**Interviewer:** Can you walk me through your invoicing process?

**David:**  
I used to use Excel, but now I mostly use a mix of Word documents and email. After a shoot, I’ll create an invoice listing the services provided, number of photos, pricing, and any extras like editing or prints. It’s usually pretty straightforward, but I’ve had clients who ask for changes mid-project, which means I have to rewrite everything. I try to keep a folder organized with all my invoices, but sometimes they get lost in my inbox or on my computer.

**Interviewer:** What’s your single biggest frustration?

**David:**  
Clients not paying on time — and then I have to chase them down. It’s stressful, especially during busy seasons when I’m juggling multiple shoots. Also, I hate having to format invoices every time — it takes time away from what I actually enjoy doing: taking pictures.

**Interviewer:** If this tool could do one magical thing, what would it be?

**David:**  
A way to generate invoices instantly from a photo gallery or project summary. Maybe even a smart estimate tool that predicts pricing based on shoot type or location.

**Interviewer:** What integrations are essential?

**David:**  
Stripe for payments, QuickBooks for accounting, and maybe a cloud storage option like Dropbox or Google Drive. I also use Instagram and Facebook to promote my work, so if I could connect those somehow, that’d be great.

**Interviewer:** Would you pay $15/month? What must be included?

**David:**  
Absolutely. I’d pay $20–$30/month if it saved me even half an hour a week. Must include professional templates, easy-to-use interface, and mobile compatibility so I can send invoices while on location.

**Interviewer:** What would make you trust this tool with financial data?

**David:**  
Strong data protection, secure login, and clear communication about how my data is stored and protected. I also want to know that I can cancel anytime without hassle.

**Interviewer:** If I build one feature this month, what should it be?

**David:**  
Mobile invoice generator with a camera-based input for uploading images directly into the invoice. That would save me tons of time and make everything more seamless.
