# MVP Definition

## **Analysis Summary**

### **User Personas:**
- **Maria**: Freelance UX Designer
- **James**: Freelance Web Developer

Both are freelancers who struggle with time-consuming and error-prone invoicing processes, often relying on spreadsheets, manual templates, and email exchanges.

---

## **Feature Categorization**

### **Must-Have Features (mentioned by 3+ personas)**  
- **Customizable invoice templates**
- **Automatic invoice creation from tracked time (e.g., Toggl, Clockify)**
- **Email-based invoice delivery**
- **Integration with QuickBooks Online**
- **Security features (SSL, 2FA, privacy policy)**

### **Should-Have Features (mentioned by 2 personas)**  
- **Recurring invoices**
- **Mobile app support**
- **Stripe payment integration**
- **Google Drive/Dropbox integration**
- **Payment tracking & follow-up reminders**

### **Nice-to-Have Features (mentioned by 1 persona)**  
- **Integration with calendar tools (e.g., Google Calendar, Outlook)**  
- **Automated invoice generation based on calendar events or project management tools**

---

## **MVP Scope**

### ✅ **IN v1**
- Customizable invoice templates
- Ability to create invoices manually
- Email delivery of invoices
- Integration with QuickBooks Online
- Security certifications (SSL, 2FA)
- Basic recurring invoice support (for retainer clients)
- Mobile-responsive web interface (no native app required yet)

### ❌ **OUT v1**
- Advanced automation (e.g., calendar sync, automatic time tracking)
- Stripe integration
- Google Drive/Dropbox integration
- Follow-up reminders or payment tracking
- Multi-currency support
- Advanced reporting features

---

## **Target Price**

**$15/month** — This price point was confirmed by Maria and aligns with the value proposition of saving 2+ hours/week in admin time. It’s competitive with existing tools like Wave, FreshBooks, and Zoho Invoice.

---

## **Surprises (What Did Personas Want That Wasn't in the Brief?)**

1. **Recurring Invoices** — Maria specifically requested this feature, which wasn’t part of the initial brief but is critical for retainer-based clients.
2. **Mobile App** — Both users expressed a need for mobile functionality, especially for sending invoices on the go.
3. **Time Tracking Integration Beyond Toggl** — While Toggl was mentioned, both users implied they'd like broader compatibility with other time-tracking tools.
4. **Trust & Security Concerns** — Both emphasized the importance of security certifications and transparency in handling financial data — this wasn’t explicitly asked but strongly implied.
5. **Integration with Calendar Tools** — James mentioned needing calendar integration, which could be a future enhancement but is a key pain point for future expansion.

--- 

## **Conclusion**

The MVP should focus on solving the core pain points: **manual, time-consuming invoicing**, **lack of automation**, and **inconsistent billing**. It should be simple, secure, and integrative enough to meet the needs of freelancers like Maria and James without overwhelming them with complexity. The goal is to reduce their admin time and give them peace of mind around invoicing and payments.
