# Personas

**Brief:** Build an invoice generator for freelancers

## User Persona 1

**Name**: Maria — Freelance UX Designer
**Background**: 32-year-old solo freelancer with 5+ years of experience in tech startups. Works with 8-12 clients monthly, charging $150-250/hour. Operates from her home office with minimal overhead.
**Current solution**: Uses Excel templates and sends PDFs via email. Manages client relationships through Google Sheets and manually tracks payments.
**Top 3 pain points**: 
1. Spending 8+ hours monthly on administrative tasks instead of design work
2. Clients requesting last-minute changes that require re-creating entire invoices
3. Difficulty tracking which invoices are paid vs. overdue
**Must-have features**: 
1. Automated invoice generation with customizable templates
2. Client portal for viewing and paying invoices
3. Integration with accounting software like QuickBooks
**Budget**: $25-35/month
**Dealbreaker**: Lack of mobile app or poor mobile experience

## User Persona 2

**Name**: James — Freelance Web Developer
**Background**: 28-year-old freelancer running a small agency with 3 team members. Specializes in WordPress and custom web solutions. Handles 15-20 clients monthly with projects ranging from $2,000-$25,000.
**Current solution**: Uses a combination of Wave and manual Excel sheets. Creates invoices in Word, sends via email, and tracks payments in spreadsheets.
**Top 3 pain points**: 
1. Inconsistent invoicing process that leads to missed payments
2. Time spent on repetitive billing tasks that could be automated
3. Difficulty managing multiple projects and tracking time across different clients
**Must-have features**: 
1. Time tracking integration with project management tools
2. Professional invoice templates with branding options
3. Automated payment reminders and follow-ups
**Budget**: $40-60/month
**Dealbreaker**: Poor customer support or lack of multi-user access for his small team

## User Persona 3

**Name**: Lisa — Freelance Content Writer
**Background**: 35-year-old solo freelancer with 8+ years of experience in B2B content marketing. Works with 12-20 clients monthly, charging $50-100/hour. Operates primarily online with no physical office.
**Current solution**: Uses Google Docs templates and sends invoices via email. Manages her business through a combination of spreadsheets and her email inbox.
**Top 3 pain points**: 
1. Inconsistent payment schedules causing cash flow issues
2. Spending too much time creating and formatting invoices
3. Difficulty tracking which clients are consistently late with payments
**Must-have features**: 
1. Automated recurring invoicing for retainer clients
2. Simple, clean invoice templates that match her brand
3. Payment history and client tracking dashboard
**Budget**: $15-25/month
**Dealbreaker**: Complex setup process or requiring technical knowledge to use

## User Persona 4

**Name**: David — Freelance Photographer
**Background**: 42-year-old freelancer with 12+ years of experience in commercial and event photography. Works with 10-15 clients monthly, charging $500-2,000 per project. Has a small studio and employs 2 assistants.
**Current solution**: Creates invoices in Photoshop and sends via email. Manages client contracts and payments through a mix of spreadsheets and email.
**Top 3 pain points**: 
1. Clients requesting custom pricing that requires manual invoice creation
2. Difficulty managing multiple projects and tracking payment status
3. Need for professional-looking invoices that reflect his premium service
**Must-have features**: 
1. Customizable invoice templates for different service types
2. Client gallery or portfolio integration for project-based billing
3. Professional branding and design customization options
**Budget**: $35-50/month
**Dealbreaker**: Inability to handle complex pricing structures or custom billing scenarios
